About Me

I am a particle physicist, recently completed my Ph.D. in Experimental High Energy Physics at IIT Madras, as part of the CMS Collaboration at CERN. My work spans both physics analysis and detector development, with interests in particle physics, semiconductor detectors, and machine learning applications in science. I also had the opportunity to participate in the Fermilab LPC Guest & Visitor Program, contributing to the CMS Outer Tracker Upgrade. Outside of research, I enjoy volunteering in community activities such as beach-cleaning drives and campus outreach programs. In my free time, I enjoy painting and exploring new ideas through both science and creativity.

Research

Hardware Activities

I have been involved in silicon detector R&D for future high-energy physics experiments. My work includes characterization of the MALTA sensor, radiation hardness studies, test beam campaigns at CERN SPS, and investigations of detector performance under various operating conditions.

Physics Analysis

I have contributed to several physics analyses within the CMS Collaboration, including measurements of the tau anomalous magnetic moment, light-by-light scattering studies, and searches for axion-like particles using ultra-peripheral PbPb collision data.

Machine Learning

I am interested in applying machine learning techniques to particle reconstruction, detector optimization, and data analysis. I am particularly interested in ML-based approaches for tau identification, particle reconstruction, and future detector applications.
